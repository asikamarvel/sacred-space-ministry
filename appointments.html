New Client Information

What to Expect in Your First Session

Your initial consultation is an opportunity for us to get to know each other. We'll discuss:

  • What brings you to therapy at this time
  • Your personal and family history
  • Current symptoms and challenges
  • Your goals for therapy
  • Questions you have about the process
  • A collaborative treatment plan

This is also your opportunity to see if we're a good fit. Therapy works best when there's a strong therapeutic relationship.

Before Your First Appointment

To make the most of your initial session, please:

  • Complete intake forms: You'll receive these via email after booking
  • Gather insurance information: If using insurance (please inquire about our panel status)
  • Prepare questions: Write down any questions you'd like to ask
  • Consider your goals: Think about what you hope to achieve
  • Plan for privacy: Ensure you'll have a private, quiet space for telehealth sessions

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if therapy is right for me?

Therapy can be beneficial if you're experiencing emotional distress, relationship difficulties, trauma symptoms, anxiety, depression, or simply feel stuck. Our free consultation call can help you determine if our services are a good fit for your needs.

What's the difference between the initial consultation and regular therapy sessions?

The initial consultation (90 minutes) is a comprehensive assessment where we gather information, discuss your goals, and create a treatment plan. Regular therapy sessions (50-60 minutes) focus on the therapeutic work itself‚Äîprocessing, learning skills, and working toward your goals.

Do you accept insurance?

Please contact us to discuss our current insurance panel participation. We can provide superbills for out-of-network reimbursement, and we're happy to help you understand your benefits.

How long will I need to be in therapy?

The duration of therapy varies based on your individual needs, goals, and the issues being addressed. Some clients benefit from short-term therapy (8-12 sessions), while others engage in longer-term work. We'll regularly assess progress together and adjust as needed.

Is telehealth as effective as in-person therapy?

Research shows that telehealth therapy can be just as effective as in-person sessions for many concerns. It offers the added benefits of convenience, accessibility, and comfort. We use secure, HIPAA-compliant technology to ensure your privacy.

What if I need to cancel or reschedule?

We require 24-hour notice for cancellations or rescheduling. You can easily reschedule through our online system or by contacting us directly. Late cancellations or no-shows may incur a fee.

How do I prepare for a telehealth session?

Find a private, quiet space where you won't be interrupted. Ensure you have a reliable internet connection and a device with a camera and microphone. Test your technology a few minutes before your session. Have water and tissues nearby.

What if I'm in crisis?

If you're experiencing a mental health emergency, please call 911 or go to your nearest emergency room. You can also contact the National Suicide Prevention Lifeline at 988 or text "HELLO" to 741741 for the Crisis Text Line. Our services are not for crisis intervention.
